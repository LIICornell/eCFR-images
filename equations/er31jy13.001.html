Computing the index rate

The index rate that equals the simple-interest money market yield on an actual/360 basis is computed as follows:

r = D 1 − Δ T 360 D

where D is the discount rate (or auction high rate), and Δ T represents the number of days from (and including) the issue date of the 13-week bill to (but excluding) the maturity date of the 13-week bill. In the table above, r = 0.095 % 1 − 91 360 × 0.095 % = 0.095022819%.
